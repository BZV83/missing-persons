Our mission is to end human trafficking in Utah by educating and enabling its citizens to take action when necessary

Human trafficking is a modern-day form of slavery involving the illegal trade of people for exploitation or commercial gain.

Learn how YOU can help

Recognize

The first step to combatting human trafficking is recognizing who is most susceptible, and when/where they are most at risk. Here are some common indicators of victims of human trafficking:

  • Disconnection from loved ones
  • Lack of freedom, often caused by an over-imposing older figure
  • Restricted communication and fearful demeanor
  • Lack of personal possessions and documentation
  • Juvenile or commercial exchange of sexual favors
  • Minor injuries, including cuts and bruising

This is not an extensive list, and it often takes multiple indicators to be strong evidence of sex trafficking. However, recognizing these and other common signs of sex trafficking can help you know when you have the opportunity to act and help.

Respond

After identifying possible instances of human trafficking, it is time to take action! However, it is vital that you do not confront the trafficker yourself. Here are resources to contact to get yourself or those you know the help they need:

  • Call 911 and report the situation
  • Contact the National Human Trafficking Hotline at 888-373-7888
  • Text BEFREE to 233733
  • Live chat at humantraffickinghotline.org
  • Leave a tip at the Utah Human Trafficking Tip Line by calling 801-200-3443

Recover

Even after being removed from threatening situations, recovering from the trauma of human trafficking is no small task. However, programs and communities have been formed to assist victims in various aspects of daily life. Below are a few examples, both local and national:

  • Consider donating to Refuge for Women, the nation's largest housing program for those recovering from trafficking situations.
  • The Victims of Crime Act (VOCA) is designed to create positivity from negativity. They have resources to help any who have been victims of crime.
  • GenerateHope is a safe place for survivors of sex trafficking to heal and find restoration in long-term housing and trauma-informed therapy, education, and vocational support.
  • The Trafficking In Persons (TIP) Program at the Asian Association of Utah (AAU), Utah's largest federally funded program tailored to serve victims of all forms of trafficking regardelss of age, gender, and immigration status.

Repeat

If you or someone close to you aren't directly affected by trafficking, it can be hard to grasp the magnitude of the issue. Estimated at a $32 billion per year industry, millions of men, women, and children are trafficked in countries around, including the U.S. Although seemingly insurmountable, there are things anyone can do to assist in the worldwide effort to abolish this blatant breach of human dignity:

  • Volunteer and support anti-trafficking efforts in your community.
  • Meet with or write to your local, state, and federal elected officials to let them know you care about combating human trafficking and ask what they are doing to address it.
  • Be well-informed. Set up a web alert to receive current human trafficking news.
  • Organize a fundraiser and donate the proceeds to an anti-trafficking organization.
  • Use your social media platforms to raise awareness about human trafficking, using the following hashtags: #endtraffickingutah or #betheoneutah. Follow local, state, and national anti-trafficking social media accounts.
  • Become a mentor to a young person or someone in need.
